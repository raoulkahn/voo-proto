Current State

Where we can improve.

These observations came from going through the onboarding as a real new user — no internal context, no prior knowledge of the product. The intent is constructive.

Opportunity — Expectation Setting
The homepage imagery creates a video expectation the product doesn't yet deliver.

The hero section shows two people on a video call with the CTA "Try your first interview free." New users — reasonably — arrive expecting a video experience. When they land in a text-based coaching session instead, there's a moment of confusion before they understand the value. This is an easy fix with a meaningful impact on first impressions.

"I thought my first free interview was going to be video, not non-video."
Opportunity — Hidden Value
The resume upload's impact is never communicated to the user.

Users can start a mock interview without uploading a resume — and many will, because there's no reason given not to. The experience without a resume is significantly less personalised. The product has an 80% improvement stat that's never shown to the user. Surfacing this at the right moment could be the single highest-leverage change in the entire onboarding flow.

Users who skip the resume upload may blame the product for generic questions — not their own decision to skip.
Warning — Orientation
Users don't know which mode is right for them.

Intervoo has at least four distinct modes: spoken practice, text coaching, video mock interview, and Live Copilot. These serve very different use cases and user states. Right now there's no guidance about which to choose or when. A user preparing for a phone screen tomorrow needs something very different from someone building skills over three weeks.

The product forces users to make a decision they don't have enough context to make.
Bug — Field Mapping
The welcome email and app screen greet the user with their job role, not their name.

After completing onboarding, both the app and the welcome email greet the user with their job role title instead of their name — "Product, your starting point is here." This appears to be a field mapping bug where the role is being substituted for the name. It's a small technical fix but the timing makes it impactful — it's the user's very first personalised touchpoint, and it's incorrect.

"Product, your starting point is here." — actual welcome email received after sign-up
Warning — Fabricated Insight
The score screen claims a "weakest dimension" before the user has done anything.

The post-signup screen shows "Your weakest dimension is Structure" with a score of 0/850. The user hasn't answered a single question. This data is fabricated — and users who notice will immediately distrust the product's other scores and insights. Personalization that isn't real is worse than no personalization at all.

Never show a personalised insight unless it's earned through real data.
Insight — What's Working
The new onboarding flow is better than it was — the bones are right.

The step-by-step structure, the role selection, and the question generation are solid foundations. The product genuinely works well once users get past onboarding. The goal isn't to rebuild from scratch — it's to fix the critical first-impression moments so users arrive with the right expectations and the right setup to have a great first session.

The product deserves better onboarding. The improvements are targeted and achievable.
Competitive Context

The landscape has shifted.

Users arriving at Intervoo are likely already aware of Final Round AI and similar tools. Understanding how they position themselves clarifies how Intervoo should differentiate.

COMPETITOR · FINAL ROUND AI

"Crack every interview with Real-Time AI Assistant"

Final Round AI's pitch is explicit: instant, undetectable answers during live interviews — "completely invisible to interviewers." They've built their entire brand around the shortcut. It's gaining traction, especially with users who are desperate.

INTERVOO'S OPPORTUNITY

"Practice until you don't need a cheat sheet."


Final Round sells a shortcut. Intervoo sells genuine readiness. These are completely different value propositions. Intervoo's user is arguably more valuable — they have integrity, want real improvement, and will stick around because skill-building is ongoing. Intervoo should lean into this contrast, not compete on AI answer speed.

Strategic Note
The Live Copilot feature should be de-emphasised for new users.

Intervoo does have a live AI assistant feature similar to Final Round. Surfacing this prominently during onboarding risks muddying the brand story — "are we the ethical practice app or the cheat tool?" New users should first experience the preparation and coaching side of the product. The Copilot can be discovered later.

Proposed Solution

A new onboarding flow.

The goal is a progressive commitment model — each step gives the user something valuable and earns the right to ask for more. Users make decisions along the way rather than being dropped into a confusing starting state.

1
Entry Point

Homepage — set honest expectations

Reframe the hero away from video-first. Lead with the outcome: "Practice until the interview feels easy." Show the product's text, spoken, and video modes clearly. The CTA becomes "Start your free practice session" — no "interview" language that implies video.

2
Sign Up

Quick sign-up — then immediately earn trust

Minimal friction: name, email, password. No resume upload yet — don't ask for too much before the user has seen value. But the next step comes immediately after, before they're dropped into the product.

3
⭐ Key Moment — Resume Upload

Upload your resume — with a compelling reason

Before the user starts anything, show them exactly why this matters: "Users who upload their resume get questions 80% more tailored to their background." Make upload the obvious choice. Include a clear "skip for now" in smaller text — no walls. Note: signing up is required to upload, but there's no charge yet.

4
⭐ Key Moment — Question Preview

Preview your personalised questions

Before the user commits to a session, show them 3–5 of the questions that have been generated for their specific role, company, and background. This is the aha moment — "these questions are actually about me." Seeing good questions makes users want to practice. This is the hook the current flow completely skips.

5
Mode Selection

Choose how you want to practice — with guidance

Present the three modes with context about when each is useful: Text coaching (anytime, anywhere — good for commuting or low-pressure practice), Spoken practice (when you want to work on delivery), Video mock interview (when you're ready to simulate the real thing). Don't make the user guess.

6
Session

Start the session — no surprises

The user arrives knowing exactly what they signed up for, with their resume uploaded and their questions personalised. The first session experience becomes dramatically better because the setup was done right.

Prioritised Recommendations

What to fix, in order.

Not everything needs to happen at once. These are ordered by impact and urgency.

P1 — Immediate
🐛

Fix the name/role bug

The "Product, your starting point is set" greeting is a quick fix with outsized first-impression impact. The welcome email also needs to be updated to not just mirror the app screen.

P1 — Immediate
📄

Add resume upload nudge before first session

Surface the 80% stat. Make upload the obvious path. One screen, one message. This is the single highest-leverage change in the product right now.

P1 — Immediate
🎯

Remove fabricated "weakest dimension" score

Don't show a personalised insight until there's real data to back it up. Replace with a neutral baseline message: "Complete your first session to see your starting score."

P2 — Next Sprint
👀

Add question preview before session start

Show 3–5 generated questions before the user commits to a session. This is the product's best sales pitch — let users experience the personalisation before they're in the session.

P2 — Next Sprint
🏠

Reframe the homepage hero

Remove or contextualise the video-call imagery. Lead with the preparation and skill-building angle, not the mock interview format. Fix the expectation mismatch at the source.

P3 — Consider
🗺️

Add mode guidance during onboarding

Help users understand the difference between text, spoken, and video practice — and which is right for their situation. A single contextual screen reduces decision paralysis significantly.

Adjacent Opportunity

Mobile unlocks a new use case.

📱

The user who can't do video right now

A significant portion of users will want to practice during a commute, lunch break, or waiting room — situations where video isn't possible. A mobile web experience focused on text coaching and question review addresses this directly. This isn't a cut-down version of the product; it's a new context where the product is genuinely useful. The onboarding improvements above should be designed with mobile in mind from the start — particularly the question preview and resume upload steps, which translate well to a mobile-first layout.
